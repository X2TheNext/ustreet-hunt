🍪 U Street Passport · July 11, 2026

Outreach Guide

Every script, ask, and talking point you need to lock in each stop before July 11. Know exactly what to say, what to ask for, and what the yes looks like.

The play: You're not selling ad space. You're inviting them into a cultural moment that's already happening on their block. They're not sponsoring you — they're being included in DC history. Lead with that energy.

📞 Cold Outreach Script (In-Person or Phone)

1
The Hook (10 seconds)
"Hey, my name is Xavier. I'm the founder of ATCHÉ — I'm putting together a one-day cultural experience on U Street on July 11th called the U Street Passport. We're working with [nearby business] and a few other spots on the corridor. I'd love 3 minutes of your time."
2
The Concept (30 seconds)
"Basically — it's a scavenger hunt meets cultural corridor experience. Participants register on their phone, hit stops along U Street, collect stamps by entering a secret word from your staff, and compete on a live leaderboard for prizes. No app download needed — just a mobile site. We're expecting 150–300 people to walk this corridor in one day, July 11th."
3
Their Role (20 seconds)
"For your business specifically — we'd list you as a checkpoint stop on the app and the map. Players who come through your door get a perk from you — something simple like a free side, 10% off, a free drink. In exchange you get foot traffic from every participant who's actively looking for your location."
4
Handle the "How much?" question
Businesses can participate two ways — product/perk only (free, just offer a deal) or paid sponsorship ($100+ for extra visibility, branding on the app, etc.).
"There's actually no cash required to be a checkpoint stop. All we ask is that you offer passport holders a small perk when they check in — something you're comfortable with. We handle everything else: the tech, the marketing, the social push. We have a paid tier if you want your logo on the app and dedicated promo, but that's optional."
5
The Yes / Close
"I can send you a one-pager with all the details. All I need from you today is — are you open on July 11th, and is there a perk you'd be comfortable offering? That's literally the only two things I need to get you on the map."
6
If they say "let me think about it"
"Totally understand. I'll text you the sponsor page right now — it has the full breakdown. We're finalizing the stop list this week so I want to lock you in before we close it. Can I follow up Thursday?"

🚫 Objection Handling
  • "We're busy on Saturdays." — That's exactly why we want you. Busy means traffic. Passport holders are coming in with intention — they're not random walk-ins, they're people who specifically came to your spot.
  • "How do we know the code won't get shared?" — Each word is unique to your location. The app validates against your specific secret word. If someone shares it, they're just advertising your spot — that's still a win.
  • "We don't do discounts." — The perk doesn't have to be a discount. It can be anything — a free water, a sticker, a branded cup, priority seating. Whatever's easy for you.
  • "What does ATCHÉ get out of it?" — Brand building. We're documenting everything for content. We're not charging the participants either. This is a cultural build — we make money through sponsors and future activations, not this event.
  • "We need to talk to the manager / owner." — Perfect. When can I come by to meet them? Or can I send you the one-pager to pass along?
Approach tip: For each stop, the key ask is always the same — (1) confirm they're open July 11, (2) lock in their perk offer, (3) get a contact name and number. Don't overthink it.

Bad Brains Corner
Near U St corridor / Columbia Rd area
🏛️ Cultural Landmark · +15 pts
The Story
Bad Brains formed in DC in 1976 — four Black teenagers who invented hardcore punk. H.R., Dr. Know, Darryl Jenifer, Earl Hudson. They were banned from half the venues they tried to play. They didn't care. The Bad Brains lightning bolt is one of the most iconic symbols in music history, and it was born right here.
  • Place a passport marker at or near this location
  • Word: BADBRAINS — say it with the energy it deserves
  • Symbol challenge: "Bad Brains were pioneers of what genre?" — hardcore punk / reggae fusion
  • This is the most shareable moment on the route — market it as such
  • Consider reaching out to HR or Bad Brains management about acknowledgment / social co-sign

Duke Ellington Birthplace
Near 2129 Ward Pl NW
🏛️ Cultural Landmark · +15 pts
Why This Matters
Duke Ellington was born April 29, 1899, just blocks from U Street. He grew up hearing this neighborhood before he became one of the most important composers in American history — 3,000+ compositions, 50 years leading his orchestra. The man who made jazz an art form started right here.
  • Place or post a passport marker at this corner
  • Word: DUKE
  • Symbol challenge: "Duke Ellington's hometown sound was…" — DC's Black Broadway jazz scene
  • Photo opp — players will want to mark this one. Design a caption prompt: "Standing where Duke started. #UStreetPassport"
